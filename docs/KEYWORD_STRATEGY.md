# Keyword strategy

This is an intent framework, not a claim about search volume. Validate demand and language in Search Console, Keyword Planner and target-market research before prioritizing investment.

## Intent groups

- Transactional retail: buy footballs online, match football, training football, futsal ball, kids football size 3/4, soccer balls online.
- Commercial investigation: best football for training, match vs training football, hand stitched vs machine stitched, football for grass or artificial turf.
- B2B and wholesale: wholesale footballs, bulk soccer balls, football supplier for clubs/schools/academies, distributor football supply.
- Custom/OEM: custom logo footballs, personalized club footballs, private-label football manufacturer, OEM soccer ball manufacturer.
- Manufacturing trust: Sialkot football manufacturer, football manufacturing process, football quality control, football testing and packaging.
- Informational: football size guide, futsal ball rebound, football care and inflation, international football shipping.
- Market language: “soccer ball” for the USA/Canada and “football” for the UK/Europe, refined through local research rather than simple replacement.

## Page-type rules

One primary intent is assigned per canonical page. Categories target product-class intent; products target exact verified product intent; guides answer comparison/how-to intent; wholesale/private-label pages target organization and sourcing intent. Avoid creating near-duplicate pages for keyword variants.

Use clear language in titles, H1s, introductions, links and image descriptions where it fits naturally. Do not repeat phrases mechanically, hide text, generate doorway markets or claim certifications, stock, prices or locations that are not documented.

## Prioritization

1. Verify catalogue and commercial policies.
2. Publish strong category and custom/wholesale pages.
3. Publish reviewed buying guides that answer real pre-sale questions.
4. Measure impressions, qualified inquiries and assisted conversions.
5. Expand market/language pages only when translation, local value and operational support exist.
